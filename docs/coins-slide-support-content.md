## 6. Short Explanation For Slides

- OOF scoring is same-family: Logistic Regression uses balanced Logistic Regression for OOF scoring; SVM uses balanced SVM. The suspiciousness score follows the same boundary geometry as the final model.
- Labels are not modified. Suspicious majority rows remain majority-labeled; COINS only changes their training weight and adds synthetic minority rows.
- CWMS and MSBS use the same OOF score in complementary ways: CWMS suppresses high-score majority rows, while MSBS uses high-score majority rows as boundary seeds for minority synthesis.
- COINS is boundary repair, not general noise cleaning. It targets hidden-minority asymmetric noise where mislabeled minority points erode the minority side of the decision boundary.
- The examples above are for slide intuition only. They are not benchmark results.

## 7. Speaker Notes

Use this framing for a concise explanation:

1. Hidden-minority noise makes true minority cases appear as majority during training.
2. A same-family OOF scorer asks: "Which majority-labeled rows look minority-like to the model family we will actually deploy?"
3. CWMS reduces the force of those suspicious majority-labeled rows without relabeling them.
4. MSBS uses observed minority anchors and suspicious majority seeds to synthesize minority support near the damaged boundary.
5. The final LR or SVM sees original labels, reduced suspicious-majority weights, and new minority-side boundary support.
